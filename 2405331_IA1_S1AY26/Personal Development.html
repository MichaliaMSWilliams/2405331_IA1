Chapter 4 – Achieve Your Goals

In this chapter, I learned that successful people do not just wish for things they set clear, specific goals. Writing down goals makes them real and gives direction to my life. Goals should include long-term, mid-term, and short-term plans so that each small step moves me closer to my bigger vision.

This chapter made me reflect deeply on my own life. I realize that although I have dreams, I do not always write them down clearly. I want to build a strong career in technology and education. Instead of just saying “I want to be successful,” I should set measurable goals such as improving my programming skills, earning certifications, and gaining practical experience. Discipline and planning are necessary if I want to achieve my ambitions.

Chapter 5 – The Proper Use of Time

This chapter focuses on how successful people use their time wisely. Time is one of the most valuable resources because once it is gone, it cannot be replaced. I must spend more time on activities that help me grow rather than on distractions.

I sometimes waste time on social media or unproductive activities. If I truly want to grow academically and professionally, I must manage my time better. I can create a daily schedule, prioritize my schoolwork, practice my skills, and limit distractions. The way I use my time today will determine my future success.

Chapter 6 – Surround Yourself with the Best People

I learned that the people I surround myself with influence my mindset, habits, and future. Positive, ambitious, and disciplined individuals push me to grow, while negative influences can limit my potential.

I want to associate myself with people who are motivated and focused on their goals. As someone involved in leadership and extracurricular activities, I also have a responsibility to be a positive influence on others. By choosing strong friendships and mentors, I can grow into a better version of myself and stay focused on my goals.

Chapter 7 – Be a Lifelong Learner

This chapter emphasizes the importance of continuous learning. Education does not stop after school; personal development should be ongoing. Reading books and learning from successful individuals are powerful habits.

I value education and self-improvement, but I must go beyond what is required in school. I can read more books related to technology and leadership, take online courses, and seek mentorship. If I commit to lifelong learning, I will remain adaptable and competitive in my future career.

Overall Reflection

Chapters 4–7 taught me that success is intentional. It requires clear goals, proper time management, positive associations, and continuous learning. These principles require discipline and consistency.

My future depends on the daily decisions I make now. By applying these lessons, I can become more focused, productive, and prepared for the opportunities ahead. This reading has motivated me to take greater responsibility for my growth and to actively design the future I desire.
